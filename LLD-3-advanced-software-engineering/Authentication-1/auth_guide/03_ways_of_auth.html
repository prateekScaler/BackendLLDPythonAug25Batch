Ways of Authentication

Different methods to prove "I am who I say I am"

The Three Authentication Factors

All authentication methods fall into three categories:

üß†

Something You KNOW

Password, PIN, Security questions

üì±

Something You HAVE

Phone (OTP), Hardware token, Smart card

üë§

Something You ARE

Fingerprint, Face, Voice, Retina

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) combines two or more of these factors. For example: Password (know) + OTP on phone (have) = 2FA.

Common Authentication Methods

Method Factor Pros Cons Used By
Password Know Simple, familiar Can be forgotten, stolen, weak Almost everywhere
OTP (SMS/Email) Have No password to remember Requires network, SIM swap attacks Banks, WhatsApp
OTP (App-based) Have More secure than SMS Need app installed Google, GitHub
Biometrics Are Convenient, hard to forge Can't change if compromised Phone unlock, airports
Magic Link Have Passwordless, simple UX Email access required Slack, Notion
Hardware Key Have Very secure, phishing-proof Can be lost, cost Google employees, Banks
