Logging & Monitoring Guide

From print() to Production Observability

Why This Matters

When your code runs on your laptop, you can use print() to debug. But what happens when your code runs on 100 servers, handling 10,000 requests per second, at 3 AM when you're asleep?

The Reality: You can't SSH into every server and tail logs. You can't add print statements to production. You need a system that captures, aggregates, and alerts you about problems automatically.

The Knight Capital Disaster (2012)

Knight Capital lost $440 million in 45 minutes due to a software bug. Their monitoring system showed unusual activity, but by the time humans noticed, they had executed 4 million trades. Better alerting could have stopped the bleeding in minutes, not 45.
